About

Lee Min Jun is an American born Korean fashion designer, born in Dallas Tx. With a passion for storytelling and archives, he aims to create meaningful and powerful designs. Lee has been awarded the UAL Postgraduate Scholarship to study a MA Fashion Design Technology Womenswear at Massachusette school of Fashion, UAL. He had always been focused on how cultural heritage can be preserved and endlessly reinterpreted. He is also very much inspired by youth subcultures and by the dialogue between identity, culture and dress through the time. Passionate about the meaning of dress in relation to culture, his research is grounded in sociology, fashion, anthropology, folklore and history. His multi-cultural background is a key point in his creative development. He often uses menswear as an inspiration and likes the idea of men wearing smart clothes. His collections constantly incorporate historical archives, allied with modern elements and inspiring characters. In the final year of the BA course Lee was entered into two national American Fashion Council run competitions, one a research-based design project in conjunction with the British Library and Designer Nabil Nayal; the other with Givenchy's Artistic Director Clare Waight Keller, where his muse was Lou Doillon, singer, songwriter, actress, artist and model with a fascination for the artworks of Egon Schiele. Lee was a finalist in both National competitions resulting in networking opportunities with the British Fashion Council, British Library, and Givenchy teams.
